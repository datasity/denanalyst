How We Work

Our Research Process

A rigorous, transparent, and practical approach to every project — from initial scoping to published findings.

01

Problem Scoping

We begin by defining the research question with precision. Who is affected? What decisions will this data inform? What does success look like? Good scoping prevents wasted effort and ensures every data point collected has a purpose.

02

Stakeholder Engagement

Before fieldwork begins, we engage with the communities, institutions, and decision-makers who will use the findings. This shapes our methodology and ensures the research is grounded in reality — not assumptions.

03

Data Collection

We use a mix of field surveys, secondary data, and structured interviews. All collection methods are designed for accuracy, repeatability, and ethical compliance. Contributors are trained and supervised throughout.

04

Cleaning & Validation

Raw data is cleaned, validated, and cross-checked for consistency. We document every transformation so our findings are fully reproducible. No shortcuts here — clean data is the foundation of credible analysis.

05

Analysis & Modelling

Depending on the project, we apply descriptive statistics, regression analysis, or predictive modelling. The method follows the question — not the other way around. We avoid analytical overkill when simple clarity serves better.

06

Insight Communication

Findings are translated into clear reports, dashboards, and presentations. We write for decision-makers, not just data professionals. Every output includes concrete recommendations alongside the findings.

07

Publication & Impact

All completed projects are published openly as public insight reports. We follow up with stakeholders to track uptake and measure the real-world impact of the research. Data is only valuable when it changes something.
